The Demand Correction

Specialty food brands that depend on co-packers plan production reactively. When a stockout happens, the weeks of zero sales suppress the trailing velocity average. The suppressed average becomes the next forecast, which produces a smaller reorder, which shortens the buffer, which makes the next stockout more likely.

The fix is correcting the data before forecasting. Out-of-stock weeks carry no demand signal — they carry silence. Replacing that silence with a rolling-median baseline adjusted for seasonality breaks the loop.

The corrected demand estimate raises the replenishment signal and produces an earlier decision deadline. Without correction, the brand plans to the suppressed number and repeats the cycle.
